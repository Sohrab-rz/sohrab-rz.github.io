Research Projects

I work at the intersection of dynamical systems, control theory, and data-driven / AI methods.

Representative Work

Nonparametric Policy Improvement in Continuous Action Spaces via Expert Demonstrations

Authors: Agustin Castellano, Sohrab Rezaei, Jared Markowitz, Enrique Mallada

Venue: Reinforcement Learning Journal, vol. 6, pp. 1158–1179; presented at the Reinforcement Learning Conference (RLC)

Problem: Classical policy improvement theory does not extend cleanly to continuous action spaces with function approximation.

Approach: A data-driven nonparametric policy built from expert demonstrations, with value lower bounds and monotonic improvement.

Outcome: A policy improvement framework and optimization algorithm with high-probability performance guarantees.

RLJ PDF OpenReview

Data-Driven Koopman Predictive Control for Frequency Regulation of Power Systems using Black-Box IBRs

Authors: Sohrab Rezaei, Xiaomo Wang, Sijia Geng

Venue: arXiv:2604.02251 [eess.SY]

Problem: Model uncertainty in inverter-based resources complicates secondary frequency regulation in power systems.

Approach: Data-driven Koopman predictive control using lifted input-output data and Willems' fundamental lemma.

Outcome: Effective frequency regulation without a parametric model, benchmarked against DeePC.

Selected Directions

Complex Dynamical Systems

Problem: Understanding structure in nonlinear, high-dimensional dynamics.

Approach: Data-driven modeling and structural analysis.

Outcome: Compact representations that support control design.

Control Theory

Problem: Stabilization and performance guarantees under uncertainty.

Approach: Modern control methods with system-theoretic constraints.

Outcome: Robust controllers for complex dynamical systems.

AI-Enhanced Modeling & Control

Problem: Bridging theory and practice using learned components.

Approach: Data-driven and AI-based methods for modeling/control.

Outcome: Next-generation methods for safer and smarter control.
